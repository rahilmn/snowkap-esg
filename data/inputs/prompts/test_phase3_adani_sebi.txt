SEBI imposes ₹275 crore penalty on Adani Power for alleged disclosure lapses in related-party transactions

Market regulator SEBI on Monday issued a show-cause notice to Adani Power Ltd with an interim penalty of ₹275 crore, citing material non-disclosure of related-party transactions between FY22-FY25 amounting to approximately ₹3,400 crore. The order directs the company to remedy the disclosures within 60 days and warns of further action including potential debarment of senior executives from the securities market.

In its 42-page order, SEBI said the company failed to disclose a series of transactions involving Adani Energy Solutions and Adani Ports that should have been flagged under Regulation 23 of the SEBI LODR regulations. The regulator cited BRSR Principle 5 (transparency) and Principle 8 (responsible policies) disclosures as inconsistent with the transactions identified.

Adani Power's stock fell 8% in intraday trade following the order, erasing approximately ₹24,500 crore in market capitalisation. Bond spreads on the company's outstanding dollar paper widened by 32 basis points, according to Bloomberg terminal data. S&P Global Ratings placed the company on negative credit watch, citing governance concerns similar to the Vedanta 2020 escalation.

The order comes at a challenging time for the Adani Group, which reported consolidated revenue of ₹45,000 crore in FY25 for the power business and which faces BRSR filing deadline on May 30 this year. Institutional investors including Norway's sovereign wealth fund and CalPERS are reviewing their positions. Analysts at Macquarie noted that cost of capital could rise by 40-60 basis points if the penalty is confirmed on appeal, impacting near-term capex plans of ₹8,000 crore over FY26-FY27.

A spokesperson for the company said the order will be challenged at the Securities Appellate Tribunal within the statutory window. Industry observers expect a resolution timeline of 12-18 months based on precedent cases.
